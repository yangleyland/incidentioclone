This article provides step by step instructions for setting up Panther as an alert source within incident.io.

## Instructions

1. Head over to the [Alerts](https://app.incident.io/~/alerts/sources) section in your incident.io dashboard

![](/images/help-centre/adding-panther-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-1.png)

2. Select the sources tab at the top of the page

![](/images/help-centre/adding-panther-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-2.png)

3. Press the 'New alert source' button

![](/images/help-centre/adding-panther-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-3.png)

4. Search for 'Panther' and click continue to create the alert source

![](/images/help-centre/adding-panther-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-4.png)

5. Switch over to your Panther dashboard, and select 'Alert Destinations' under the 'Configure' section

![](/images/help-centre/adding-panther-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-5.png)

6. If you already have another alert destination set up, click the 'Create New' button in the top right hand corner. Otherwise, click 'Add your first Destination'.

![](/images/help-centre/adding-panther-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-6.png)

![](/images/help-centre/adding-panther-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-7.png)

7. Choose 'Custom Webhook' from the list of destinations

![](/images/help-centre/adding-panther-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-8.png)

8. Copy the Webhook URL provided to you after you created a Panther alert source in the incident.io dashboard, and make sure to add a custom HTTP header for 'Authorization'. The value for this header is provided to you in the incident.io dashboard, alongside the Webhook URL.

![](/images/help-centre/adding-panther-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-9.png)

9. Click 'Add Destination' and then 'Send Test Alert' to test your integration. You should be able to see a test alert in your incident.io dashboard!

![](/images/help-centre/adding-panther-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-10.png)

![](/images/help-centre/adding-panther-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-11.png)
