This article provides step by step instructions for setting up SolarWinds AppOptics as an alert source within incident.io.

## Instructions

1. Head over to the [Alerts](https://app.incident.io/~/alerts/sources) section in your incident.io dashboard

![](/images/help-centre/adding-solarwinds-appoptics-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-1.png)

2. Select the sources tab at the top of the page

![](/images/help-centre/adding-solarwinds-appoptics-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-2.png)

3. Press the 'New alert source' button

![](/images/help-centre/adding-solarwinds-appoptics-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-3.png)

4. Search for 'AppOptics' and click continue to create the alert source

![](/images/help-centre/adding-solarwinds-appoptics-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-4.png)

5. Head over to the AppOptics dashboard, and navigate to *Settings* → *Metric Settings* → *Notification Services*

![](/images/help-centre/adding-solarwinds-appoptics-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-5.png)

6. Click on *Webhooks* to add a configuration

![](/images/help-centre/adding-solarwinds-appoptics-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-6.png)

7. Enter a name for your webhook, and the URL provided from your incident.io dashboard, and click *Add* .

![](/images/help-centre/adding-solarwinds-appoptics-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-7.png)

7. Now, you can configure your alerts to be sent to incident.io, by navigating to the *Notifications* tab on any alert you want to connect, and enabling the notification service you just created.

![](/images/help-centre/adding-solarwinds-appoptics-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-8.png)

8. Finally, you can test the connection by clicking *Test Fire* on the right hand side of the page.

![](/images/help-centre/adding-solarwinds-appoptics-as-an-alert-source/screenshot-9.png)
